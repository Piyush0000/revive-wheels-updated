Frequently Asked Questions

Is EV conversion legal in India?
Yes, EV conversions are legal in India when performed by certified workshops. The converted vehicle needs to be certified by an approved testing agency (like ARAI or ICAT) and registered as an electric vehicle with your local RTO. We handle all the certification and paperwork as part of our conversion service.
How long does the conversion process take?
The conversion typically takes 3-6 weeks depending on the package you choose and the complexity of your vehicle. This includes the physical conversion, testing, and certification process. We provide a loaner vehicle during this period for basic packages and above.
What is the lifespan of a converted EV?
Our battery systems are designed for 1500-2000 charge cycles while maintaining 80% capacity. For average usage, this translates to 8-10 years of service. The electric motor typically lasts 15+ years with minimal maintenance. We offer battery replacement programs when the time comes.
Can I convert any vehicle to electric?
Most vehicles under 1500kg can be converted, but some are better suited than others. During our free assessment, we evaluate your vehicle's structure, weight distribution, and available space for batteries. Generally, lighter vehicles with manual transmissions convert most successfully.
What about servicing and repairs after conversion?
We provide training to select service centers across major cities to handle maintenance on converted EVs. The electric drivetrain requires significantly less maintenance than combustion engines - primarily just brake fluid, coolant, and tire rotations. We also offer annual checkup packages.
